CanSat

Designing a satellite the size of a soda can to simulate real space missions from launch to data recovery.

Live Demo View Code

About the Project

The CanSat competition is an international student engineering challenge where teams design and build a small satellite that fits inside a soda can. The CanSat is launched by a rocket or drone to a certain altitude and then released, where it must perform a mission such as collecting atmospheric data, transmitting telemetry, or deploying a recovery system. The goal is to give students hands-on experience with real-world space engineering concepts like systems design, sensors, communications, and data analysis.

Key Features

  • Compact satellite design constrained to the size of a soda can
  • Real-time wireless telemetry transmission to a ground station
  • Onboard microcontroller and sensor integration
  • Mission simulation covering launch, deployment, and data analysis
